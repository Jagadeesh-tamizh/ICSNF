About The Event

About The Conference

International Conference on Smart Intelligent Systems, Newer Materials and Future Communication (ICSNF 2023) is a platform for researchers, scholars, and practitioners in the field of Computer Science and Engineering to present and exchange their latest findings, ideas, and advancements. We cordially invite scientists, academics, engineers, and students from universities around the globe as well as from business to submit their most recent research papers to ICSNF 2023 and to share their invaluable experiences with other scientists and academics from around the globe.

The ICSNF 2023 conference serves as an international arena for the dissemination of scientific findings and recent innovations in computer science, engineering, and technology. The conference offers the attendees the chance to interact face-to-face, to establish business or research relations and to find global partners for future collaboration.

The conference will typically cover a broad range of topics related to computer science, including but not limited to: Big Data Analytics in cloud, Quantum Computing, Block Chain Technology, Network and Cyber Security, Databases and Data Security. Presentations will be delivered in the form of keynote speech, invited talk, and research papers.

Scientific Sessions

Scientists today produce millions of research articles, preprints, grant proposals, and patents each year, leaving detailed fingerprints of the work we admire and how they come about. Access to this data catalyzes the emergence of a new multidisciplinary field called the science of science, which, by helping us to understand in a quantitative fashion the evolution of science, has the potential to unlock enormous scientific, technological, and educational value.

Scientific Sessions agenda

The hope is that both the producers and the consumers in the arena of science of science and innovation can directly engage through the invaluable feedback loop that can drastically increase the practical relevance and impact of science of science research.

Paper submission

1PAPER SUBMISSION GUIDELINES:
Abstract Submission Guidelines:
  • Abstract should include purpose of study, methodology, summary of findings.
  • Prospective authors are encouraged to submit their original, unpublished research paper.
  • It should be submitted and presented in clear English with accurate grammar and spelling of a quality suitable for publication.
  • It should be between 200-250 words in a single paragraph.

  • All abstracts should be written in MS Word format (DOC or DOCX).
  • The abstract must indicate: Title of paper, name(s) of author(s), affiliation(s), and contact details,managerial implications, and key references.
  • Submitted abstracts would undergo review by expert reviewers. Notification of accepted abstracts would be sent to the author via email.
Full Paper Submission Guidelines:
  • Manuscripts not to exceed 5,000 words each (excluding tables, figures and references) in double-column format. Manuscripts longer than 5,000 words will be returned for editing.
  • Tables should be created within the Microsoft Word document, should fit onto one A4 page and should be numbered and captioned below the table, centre aligned.
  • All figures must be inserted in a JPEG image format, within the page margins.
  • Manuscripts should contain Objective, Methods, Results and Conclusions.
  • Submissions must be plagiarism free.
2PRESENTATION GUIDELINES:
Guidelines for Oral presentation:
  • All slides should have a consistent look and feel with proper alignment.
  • Standardise the communication using graphs and charts to convey complex information.
  • Use no more than 6 words per bullet, 6 bullets per slide, or 6 word slides in a row.
  • Keep your presentation simple, clear and direct.
  • Use high-contrast, easy-to-read fonts that are common to most computers. Do not use ALL CAPS, italics, and other enhancements that clutter and distract. A good guideline is a minimum of 30-point font.
Guidelines for Poster presentation:
  • The heading of the poster should be visible and it should include topic, Author name and contact details.
  • The letters should be visible at least from approximately 2.5 meters.
  • Use simple format and more spatial layout with good color combination.
  • Use reader friendly icons and symbols such as arrows, numbers and etc.
  • Avoid much as possible abbreviations and jargons.
  • Highest clarity and visibility will attract the audience.
Guidelines for Virtual presentation:
  • Updated Chrome Browser.
  • Use as quiet an area as possible.
  • A hardline internet connection is recommended, but if unavailable, use a strong Wi-Fi connection.
  • Try and use a good headset with the microphone close to the mouth BUT away from direct-line-of-mouth to reduce “pops”.

Publication

Journal details and Publication

Steps involved in publishing a research paper:
  • Choose a conference: Select a conference that is relevant to your area of research and has a call for papers open.
  • Prepare the manuscript: Write a high-quality research paper that follows the conference guidelines for format, length, and style.
  • Submit the manuscript: Submit the manuscript through the conference submission system, along with any required attachments such as an abstract and author information.
  • Review process: The conference organizers will conduct a peer-review process to assess the quality and relevance of the submitted papers. This process may involve one or more rounds of revisions.
  • Notification of acceptance: The authors will receive notification of whether their paper has been accepted for presentation and publication at the conference.
  • Prrepare for presentation: If the paper is accepted, prepare for the presentation at the conference, including any necessary slides, posters, or handouts.
  • Attend the conference: Attend the conference and present the paper to the conference audience.
  • Finalize the publication: After the conference, the authors may be required to make any final revisions to the paper and provide the final version for publication in the conference proceedings or journal special issue.
Ethic and publication malpractise:
  • Originality: Ensure that the research paper is original and does not plagiarize the work of others.
  • Multiple submissions: Do not submit the same paper to multiple international conferences, as this is considered unethical.
  • Confidentiality: Do not disclose any confidential or proprietary information in the paper or during the presentation.
  • Accuracy: Ensure that the research paper is accurate and based on valid data and methods.
  • Conflict of interest: Disclose any conflicts of interest, such as financial or institutional affiliations, that may affect the research results.
  • Appropriate citations: Properly cite any sources used in the research paper, including previous work by others.
  • Responsibility for data: Ensure that the data used in the research is properly collected, analyzed, and reported, and that the study has been approved by any necessary ethics committees.
  • Collaboration: Acknowledge any collaborators or co-authors, and ensure that their contributions to the research are accurately represented.
Plagiarism policy
  • Zero-tolerance policy: Conferences may have a strict policy that any instance of plagiarism, regardless of the extent, will result in immediate rejection of the submission.
  • Original work requirement: Conferences may require that submissions are original work and not previously published or under consideration for publication elsewhere.
  • Proper citation: Conferences may expect all sources used in the research to be properly cited and referenced in the submission.
  • Similarity check: Conferences may use plagiarism detection software to check submissions for instances of plagiarism, including text copied from other sources or self-plagiarism.
  • Investigation process: Conferences may have a set process for investigating and verifying instances of plagiarism, including the opportunity for authors to provide a response or explanation.
  • Sanctions: Conferences may impose sanctions for instances of plagiarism, such as withdrawing an acceptance, revoking an award, or reporting the incident to relevant academic institutions or organizations.
